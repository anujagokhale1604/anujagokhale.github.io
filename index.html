Research & Publications
Core Finding
"India's CPI Granger-causes Singapore's inflation with a two-month lag (p = 0.028). Singapore's in turn Granger-causes the UK's (p = 0.039). Neither reverse direction holds. The transmission chain runs one way — emerging market supply dynamics lead the advanced economy inflation cycle. The pipe ran the other way."
Published · SSRN Working Paper · Version 2 · 2026
Cross-Country Macroeconomic Dynamics: Inflation, Growth, and Monetary Policy — India, Singapore, and the United Kingdom
Gokhale, A.A. (2026) · ssrn.com/abstract=6514338
Documents asymmetric Granger causality chain India to Singapore to UK (p = 0.028). Sectoral decomposition identifies Fuel and Light and Core manufactured goods as the transmission mechanism — not food prices — consistent with the Dominant Currency Paradigm. Episode-specific analysis reveals the India to Singapore link activates during supply-driven inflationary regimes. Employs VAR(2), Johansen cointegration, Markov-switching, and Diebold-Mariano forecasting.
Published · SSRN Working Paper · 2026
Measuring Core Inflation for Monetary Policy in Singapore: Replication and Extension of Roger & Xiong (2025)
Gokhale, A.A. (2026) · ssrn.com/abstract=6847179 · MAS Staff Paper No. 2025/1
Full replication of six core inflation measures from publicly available SingStat data. Four original extensions: structural break analysis (Chow test, 2021), real-time Diebold-Mariano forecasting horse race, novel India upstream contamination test, and Phillips curve augmentation. Finds XFE and T15 fail the upstream contamination check.

Interest 01
Dominant Currency Transmission
The sectoral decomposition confirms fuel and core manufactured goods drive the India to Singapore channel. The next question: does dollar invoicing mediate the two-month lag? Testing the DCP mechanism directly in the Asian corridor.
Interest 02
Monetary Policy and Firm Dynamics
Does the instrument of monetary tightening — exchange rate versus interest rate — determine which firms exit and which survive? Singapore and the UK tightened simultaneously but through different mechanisms. The micro consequences remain unexplored.
