Research identity
I study how economic shocks travel across borders — and what happens when they arrive.
My research sits at the intersection of monetary economics, open economy macroeconomics, and international finance. I focus on how institutional frameworks shape the transmission and persistence of inflationary shocks across economies with different monetary policy designs.
My current work asks a question that sounds simple but has a counterintuitive answer: when global inflation surges, where does it actually come from? The conventional narrative blamed advanced economy central banks. My evidence points upstream — toward emerging market supply dynamics that most commentary had not examined carefully.

The central finding
"India's CPI Granger-causes Singapore's inflation with a two-month lag (p = 0.028). Singapore's in turn Granger-causes the UK's (p = 0.039). Neither reverse direction holds. The transmission chain runs one way — emerging market supply dynamics lead the advanced economy inflation cycle."

Working paper
Cross-Country Macroeconomic Dynamics: Inflation, Growth, and Monetary Policy — India, Singapore, and the United Kingdom
Gokhale, Anuja A. (2026) · SSRN Working Paper · ssrn.com/abstract=6514338
Examines how inflation dynamics, monetary policy frameworks, and growth cycles interacted across the post-pandemic period in three structurally distinct economies. Documents near-unit inflation persistence, an asymmetric cross-country Granger causality chain, structural VAR analysis, Johansen cointegration validation, and Diebold-Mariano out-of-sample forecasting. Extended VAR projection to December 2026 with direct policy implications for the MAS S$NEER framework.

Policy interests
Exchange rate vs. interest rate regimes
Does Singapore's S$NEER framework offer a more targeted instrument for small open economies with high import dependence? The persistence differential (β = 0.97 vs 0.99) suggests it might — and the mechanism matters for ECB and BoE framework design.
Upstream attribution of inflation
When emerging market supply dynamics lead the inflation cycle, advanced economy central banks may be treating a symptom rather than a cause. This has direct implications for how the ECB interprets Eurozone inflation persistence.
Climate transition as macroeconomic risk
Transition shocks are supply-side shocks with persistent inflationary consequences — exactly the dynamics I study. How should monetary frameworks adapt as decarbonisation reshapes global commodity supply chains?
Future research agenda
Paper 2 · In progress
Monetary Tightening and Creative Destruction: Firm Dynamics Across Policy Regimes
Does exchange-rate versus interest-rate tightening produce different patterns of firm exit, productivity dynamics, and sectoral reallocation? Using sector-level data from Singapore DOS and UK ONS with a DiD design around the 2021–2022 tightening episodes.
Paper 3 · Planned
Forecast Error Variance Decomposition of Singapore CPI
Quantifying the proportion of Singapore's CPI forecast error variance attributable to India shocks versus UK/financial conditions versus domestic dynamics. A direct extension of the current VAR framework.
Longer term
Geoeconomic Fragmentation and Transmission Channel Stability
Does US-China decoupling and friend-shoring weaken or strengthen the India → Singapore → UK chain? As supply chains bifurcate, which transmission channels survive and which break?
