Methodology

The Monsoon Index operationalises the core finding of Gokhale (2026) — that India's CPI Granger-causes Singapore's CPI (p = 0.028) and Singapore's CPI in turn Granger-causes the UK's (p = 0.039), while no reverse causality holds — as a live monitoring signal.


Granger causality tests whether lagged values of one series improve the forecast of another beyond its own lags. The index runs pairwise tests across all six country-pair combinations. Persistence coefficients (β) are OLS estimates of current CPI regressed on one-period lagged CPI. The rolling signal re-estimates the India→Singapore test on each trailing 36-month window to track how the transmission relationship evolves over time.


Data sources: MAS Statistics, Reserve Bank of India DBIE, UK Office for National Statistics. Full methodology: ssrn.com/abstract=6514338
